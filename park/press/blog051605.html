Cultural Affairs Meeting on LAPD HQ
Police Headquarters Plan Goes Before Cultural Affairs Commission

This Thursday the city's Cultural Affairs Commission will be looking at the entire LAPD headquarters proposal. It is a massive project with major implications for downtown redevelopment, culturally and otherwise. The commission will be looking at all three parts of the plan:

1) The main headquarters building. This is the 500,000-square-foot, 12-story building on the old Caltrans site -- the block at 1st, 2nd, Spring and Main -- where a civic park had been planned. At its Feb 3 meeting, the commision declined to approve the plan and asked the city council to revisit its choice of this site. So far the city hasn't done so. (Because the commission didn't technically vote down the plan, by default, the city considered it concepturally approved 15 days later.)

2) The parking garage. This would be in the center of the St. Vibiana's block -- about 30 yards from the just-restored landmark. The plans made public last week show a 6-story (four above ground, two below) 500-space gagage. The entrance and exit are on Main Street and it is for police cars only.

3) The motor transport facility. This building, parked in front of the garage and fronting main street, is for the car wash, gas station and service bays for 1,000 police vehicles. It is 2-story building and the exit and entrance are onto Main Street.

To make room for the garage and motor pool, the city would claim privately held properties, dramatically reduce public parking in the immediate area, tear down the 1896 MJ Higgins Gallery building on Main where Gallery Row was founded. Also knocked off the block would be an open-air Mexican restaurant on Main and the Chou family business on Los Angeles Street, Vida Enterprises, which has more than a dozen employees.

If you care about these issues, please attend the meeting.
