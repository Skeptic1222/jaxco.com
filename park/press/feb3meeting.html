Cultural Affairs Commission Does Not Approve City's Plans for Police Headquarters on Old Caltrans Site; Hearing Continues Thursday Feb. 3

There was good news for park advocates from the Cultural Affairs Commission hearing Thursday, Jan 20: A motion to approve the police headquarters project failed on a 3-2 vote.

The decision was immediately contested by city representatives, however, and the commission passed a second motion that continues the matter to its next meeting, which will be at 9:30 a.m., Feb. 3.

The commissioners' reservations were not about the design of the building but about seeing it built on the old Caltrans site. Lacivicpark.org argued that approval of the plans would undermine the emerging arts movements in the area and squander the ideal site for a great civic park for Los Angeles.

Three commissioners spoke about the importance of making a decision that supported the larger cultural interests at stake and of the commission’ s responsibility to vote not just on the specifics of the design but on the impact of the overall project.

The commissioners asked that a city attorney be present at the next meeting and for representatives of the planning department to more fully explain why this site was chosen.
