Park Update

As we continue to fight for a park in the heart of LA, the city is moving ahead with its plans to instead build a massive new police headquarters where this park has been planned for more than a decade.

The police HQ proposal is subject to the California Environmental Quality Act (CEQA) and an Environmental Impact Report is currently being prepared by the city. Once the draft of that report is done (expected to be this summer or fall), there will be a period of public comment. After any revisions are made, the report will go before interested subcommittees of the City Council and then to the full Council. Nothing can happen to the site until then.

We continue our efforts to get the City Council to change its thinking about the site -- we were heartened when in a courageous move the city's Cultural Affairs Commission asked it to do just that. LA needs a plan that is good not just for the police, but for ordinary citizens and generations to come.

We are closely follow the Environmental Impact Report. We've filed a response to the city's initial study -- it's posted here on the site along with other information.

The city's current plan squanders the ideal location for a park that would welcome the people of Los Angeles into its center. A police HQ on this site would harm the city in other ways too -- including physically and emotionally walling off City Hall from the city itself. And it would undermine the arts, residential and business communities developing all around it. (The police project would not only takes over the old Caltrans site, but much of the block anchored by St. Vibiana's.

We hope you'll stay tuned -- and help spread the word. Elected officials, cultural and other community leaders need to know you care about this park for LA. Thanks for your support!
