Police HQ Plans Rejected
Cultural Affairs Commission Rejects Police HQ Plans

The Los Angeles Cultural Affairs Commission rejected plans for all three components of the proposed LAPD headquarters at its meeting May 19.

The motion to approve plans for the main headquarters building on the old Caltrans site failed on a 3-2 vote. Two commissioners expressed dismay at the site selection and the process that led to it, including disregard shown for the master plan for Civic Center development. (That plan calls for a civic park on the block at 1st, 2nd, Spring and Main and for rebuilding the police headquarters on the current Parker Center site.) A third commissioner voted against the headquarters plan because the design was not in keeping with positive feng shui. (Specifically, the sharp point on the north side would direct negative energy toward City Hall and those who work there.)

No vote was taken on the second and third parts of the proposal because no commissioner would second the motion to approve them. Those parts of the plan call for a parking garage and motor pool (car wash, gas station and service bays) facing Main Street on the St. Vibiana's block. Among those objecting to the plan were members of the arts community, downtown residents and property owners whose buildings would be torn down.
