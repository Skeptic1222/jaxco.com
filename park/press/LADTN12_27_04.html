Los Angeles Downtown News
Dec. 27, 2004

From a Roundup of the Year's Events in L.A.

by Kathryn Maese

A Home for LAPD: After a protracted battle over where to build a new police headquarters, the City Council in June unexpectedly approved the former Caltrans site at First and Spring streets. Earlier that month, several of the city's most powerful politicos were sparring over different proposals, with varying financial estimates being tossed around. Among the locations being considered were a 10-acre lot at First and Alameda in Little Tokyo, which residents wanted for a community center; rebuilding on the existing Parker Center site at First and Los Angeles streets; and Transamerica's Broadway Building in South Park. Fed up with the delays, Ninth District Councilwoman Jan Perry drove around Downtown with the city's Chief Legislative Analyst Ron Deaton (now moved on to the DWP) and identified five possible sites - among them the old Caltrans location. It turned out building on the site would cost the same amount as the frontrunner in Little Tokyo, and in the span of a few days, Perry managed to get Mayor Jim Hahn, Deaton and the City Council to endorse the plan. Construction on the 500,000-square-foot complex could begin in mid-2005.
