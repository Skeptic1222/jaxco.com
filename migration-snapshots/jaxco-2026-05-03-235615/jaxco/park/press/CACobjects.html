Cultural Affairs Commission Objects to Police HQ on Old Caltrans Site; Urges Council to Reconsider Decision

The Cultural Affairs Commission voted Feb. 3 to voice its serious reservations about the choice of the old Caltrans site for the new LAPD headquarters in a letter to the Los Angeles City Council.

The 5-1 decision by the commission followed an earlier vote, Jan. 20, in which a motion to approve conceptual plans for the project failed 3-2. Rather than vote again on the proposal, the commission voted to communicate its concerns to the council in the form of a letter of protest that will ask the council to reconsider its decision.

The commission’s action was welcomed by those who support long-standing city plans to create a major civic park on the block at 1st, 2nd, Spring and Main.

An assistant city attorney said it was his opinion that the commission did not have the authority to vote on the appropriateness of the site, only of the building plans. Commission members said the two could not be separated in this case.

The commission vote followed public comment in support of a park on the site and about the history of council actions in support of using it for open space.

Locating the police headquarters project on the old Caltrans site and on the adjacent St. Vibiana’s block will have an impact well beyond the buildings themselves. It will squander the ideal setting for a major civic park for Los Angeles and the nature and size of the project willf undermine the emerging arts and cultural movements downtown just as they are beginning to take root.
