James Rojas: Feb 8 message to the "newdowntown" message board
WHO PLANS FOR DOWNTOWN???
I moved downtown for the urban experience and make it livable. There
are some things I can change and other things that I cannot.
Homelessness is one I have to deal with living in downtown LA. Every
day for the past 5 years I have had to step over urine, needles and
bodies at my front door and every day we clean the mess up.

Government has destroyed the livability of downtown and s city's
urban essence. My father grew up on Bunker Hill at Temple and Grand
in the 1940's in a mixed-use building and he has lots of great
memories of it. Bunker Hill could have been the crowning glory of
downtown LA with it's old Victorians homes, gardens and hotels that
would have rivaled anything San Francisco has to offer. Yet it has
taken the CRA 50 years to redevelop Bunker Hill and another 50 to
complete it. In the meantime Bunker Hill is lifeless streets and
parking lots. This is not good planning!

Three years ago when I opened by Gallery, there were only vacant
storefronts on Spring/Main Streets. Government did not provide us
any money or tell me to do it. My partners and I had a vision and
spend countless hours of our time, not getting paid, to make it
happen. There was no "gallery row" just vision and sweat equity from
a few individuals. The gallery owners volunteer their time to make it
work. The city is not subsidizing us. The galleries bring a cultural
cache to the city and attract thousands of visitors a month to once
forgotten parts of downtown LA.

If you study DC history, which I did when I worked for the National
Capital Planning Department, the federal offices were decentralized
after World War II to places like Bethesda. DC has made a very
successful effort of building and keeping the historical housing
stock and preventing the whole city from being engulfed by government
buildings. We somehow we think by expanding civic center we are
creating a livable city.

Why should downtown LA be the dumping ground for facilities nobody
else wants in their neighborhoods? There are more jails in downtown
LA than in any other part of the city. I see more released inmates
walking from prison to streets then non-prisoners. This does not
improve property values.

Who is planning for a livable downtown LA, people who live here or
people who do not? I know as a downtown resident if I go to Echo
Park, West LA and tell those residents how to plan their community I
would be run out of the meeting.

Keep the police head quarters at Parker Center!
