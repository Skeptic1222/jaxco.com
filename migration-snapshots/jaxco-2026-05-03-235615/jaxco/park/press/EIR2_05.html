POLICE HEADQUARTERS FACILITY PLAN

BE 004-05.  Notice of Preparation and Initial Study, Police Headquarters Facility Plan, CD 9.  The City of Los Angeles will be the Lead Agency and will prepare an Environmental Impact Report (EIR) for the Police Headquarters Facility Plan (project).  The project includes the construction of a new 500,000 square-foot police headquarters facility with subterranean parking consisting of 700 parking spaces on the block bounded by 1st, Main, 2nd, and Spring Streets.  It will replace the existing police headquarters at Parker Center.  In addition, an open-space landscaped area would be provided and the new police headquarters facility would include a 350-seat auditorium, a food service component, and a helipad on the roof of the building.  An additional off-site parking structure for police personnel would be constructed on parcels mid-block south of 2nd Street, between Main and Los Angeles Streets.  The parking structure would be three levels below grade with 500 parking spaces for police personnel and 100 parking spaces for public use.  Adjacent to the south, a replacement facility for the Police Department’s Motor Transport Division would be constructed between Main Street and Werdin Place.  The replacement 28,000 square-foot facility would contain an auto repair shop with services bays, a car wash, and fuel island for the maintenance and repair of police fleet vehicles.  The proposed project would also construct a public parking structure containing three levels below grade and 300 parking spaces northwest of 1st and Judge John Aiso Streets.  At grade level, an open space area or public plaza would be provided.  Construction of the project is scheduled from mid 2006 to early 2009.

We need to know the views of your agency as to the scope and content of the environmental information that is germane to your agency’s statutory responsibilities in connection with the proposed project.  We also need to know the views and concerns of interested organizations and persons in order to properly analyze the environmental impacts of the proposed project.  Potential environmental impacts that may occur as a result of the proposed project include aesthetic, air quality, noise, and traffic impacts and impacts to cultural resources.  An analysis of these potential environmental impacts and other potential impacts that could be mitigated to a less-than-significant level is provided in an Initial Study checklist, which is attached to the Notice of Preparation or can be reviewed at the reference desks of the following libraries: Little Tokyo Branch Library at 244 South Alameda Street; Central Library at 630 West Fifth Street; or Chinatown Branch Library at 639 N. Hill Street.

Proposition Q and F Civic Center Public Safety Facilities Project

BE 006-04: Environmental Impact Report, Proposition Q and F Civic Center Public Safety Facilities Project. State Clearinghouse No. 2004031142.  The City of Los Angeles (City) is proposing to construct new and replacement public safety facilities in the downtown area of the City of Los Angeles, County of Los Angeles.  The planned improvements are projects that were approved by Los Angeles City voters as part of the Proposition F – Fire Facilities/Animal Shelter Facilities Bond Measure and Proposition Q – Citywide Public Safety Bond Measure.  The proposed project is located within Council Districts 9 and 14.

The City has prepared a draft Environmental Impact Report (EIR) in accordance with the California Environmental Quality Act (CEQA) for the proposed project.  The draft EIR describes the potential environmental impacts of the proposed project, which at the time of preparation, was known as the Public Safety Facilities Master Plan.  Upon completion of the draft EIR, changes to the project occurred.  The project now has fewer components than what was originally proposed.  However, the environmental analysis presented in the draft EIR was not modified to reflect the changes.  Therefore, when reviewing and commenting on the draft EIR, please keep in mind that the analysis is based on a larger project than what is now being proposed. 

A summary of the reduced project, which is now known as the Proposition Q and F Civic Center Public Safety Facilities project, is provided below. 

  • The project is located in the block bounded by Temple, Judge John Aiso, 1st, and Los Angeles Streets and the block bounded by Temple, Vignes, 1st, and Alameda Streets in the downtown area of Los Angeles.

  • The project includes:

    • Construction of replacement 179,000 square-foot Metro Jail containing 512 beds.  The facility would be located south of Temple and Los Angeles Streets, adjacent to Parker Center.

    • Construction of replacement two-story, 82,000 square-foot Emergency Operations Center-Police Operations Center-Fire Dispatch Center (EOC-POC-FDC) to be located southwest of Temple and Vignes Streets..

    • Construction of new EOC-POC-FDC parking structure containing three levels above grade and approximately 210 parking spaces for personnel.  Parking structure would be located southwest of Temple and Vignes Streets, adjacent to the new EOC-POC-FDC.

    • Placement of a 180-foot communications tower and accessory structure for the EOC-POC-FDC in the parking lot of the City’s Personnel Department building at 700 E. Temple Street.

    • Construction of a replacement two-story, 40,000 square-foot facility for Fire Station #4.  The new fire station would be located south of Temple and Garey Streets, adjacent to the new EOC-POC-FDC.

    • Construction of a new surface parking lot south of the new Fire Station #4 containing approximately 147 parking spaces for personnel.

    • Construction of a replacement 30,000 square-foot facility for the Medical Services Division (MSD) of the City’s Personnel Department.  The new MSD facility would be located within the new EOC-POC-FDC parking structure.

    • Vacation of Banning Street, between Alameda and Vignes Streets, and related street dedications.

    • Demolition of onsite improvements and relocation of two commercial businesses.

  • The anticipated construction schedule is from year-end 2005 to year-end 2007.

The draft EIR is available for public review and comment for a period of 45 days beginning on Tuesday, February 1, 2005 and ending on Thursday, March 17, 2005.  The public review period of the draft EIR provides an opportunity for interested persons, agencies, or organizations to comment on the adequacy of the EIR in identifying and analyzing potential significant environmental impacts and how they may be avoided or mitigated.  All comments received will become part of the official public record.
