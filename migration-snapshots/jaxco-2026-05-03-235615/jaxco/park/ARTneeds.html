Art Needs This Park

The city's plan to build a police headquarters instead of a park on the old Caltrans site -- the block at 1st, 2nd, Spring and Main -- will undermine the emerging arts movement in downtown L.A.

The block marks the start of Gallery Row, the arts initiative along Main and Spring streets that in less than a year has grown from 4 galleries to 15. Across the street is historic St. Vibiana's, built in 1876. The former cathedral -- narrowly saved from an earlier wrecking ball -- has just undergone a $6 million renovation and transformation into an arts center. On the other side of Main, the Linda Lea theater is also about to be renovated and re-opened as a film and arts venue. Works of art, art fairs and festivals in the park were to have played a key role in bringing people into the area.

Under the city's current plan, the police facility will take over the old Caltrans block and much of the block on Main where St. Vibiana's stands.

Not only would there be no park to showcase artwork and attract crowds, the building on Main where the Gallery Row concept was born -- formerly Inshalah, now MJ Higgins -- would be torn down. A major police parking structure and a motor pool where police cars are washed and serviced would be built there – next to St. Vibiana's. There would be almost no public parking left.

These cultural endeavors, allowed to thrive, will help create a lively, authentic arts district that bridges the established art museums just east and west -- MOCA, the Geffen Contemporary and the Japanese American National Museum -- and the Music Center's performing arts venues up the hill. A massive police facility on this site will permanently alter the potential of the area to develop into a commercial and pedestrian friendly zone that will help the arts community and surrounding neighborhoods succeed.

Help us get the park back on track. Visit our website, lacivicpark.org, to sign a petition in support of the park and join our on-line discussion group. And let your elected representatives and candidates for office know what you think.
